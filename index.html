Hi, I'm Tim.

And I love to write code.

Seriously, I can't believe they pay me to do it. I started programming when I was eight and sold my first application at 13. Over the years, I've funneled that passion into a successful career, crafting software for enterprises, small businesses, and everything in between

Currently, I'm a Senior Full-Stack .NET Developer at Kentucky.gov, a subsidiary of NIC. Previously, I was a Senior .NET Consultant with Software Information Systems (SIS) working as development lead on the eKASPER and eCourts applications for the Commonwealth of Kentucky. Over my many years of experience, I have had the pleasure of working with — and learning from — a number of great companies in a variety of industries. You may view my relevant work history on my résumé.

I would not be where I am today were it not for some amazing teachers and mentors along the way. Today, I strive to be a teacher and mentor to the next generation of developers. Most of my effort along those lines occurs directly within my development teams and personal network, but ocassionally I find time to write a blog article that other developers find useful. I also enjoy presenting at user groups.

Aside from code, I'm a husband, father, backyard barbeque pitmaster, amateur singer/songwriter/producer, and novice gardner. Not to toot my own horn or anything, but co-workers have described my pulled pork barbeque as "the best [they've] ever had." Just sayin'.
